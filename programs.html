Business Analysis Course Curriculum
Introduction to Business Analysis
This lesson prepares your mind as to what business analysis entails, the roles that business analyst play in an organization and the agile perspective. At the end of the topic you should have a clear understanding of the expectations of a business analyst.
Key Concepts In Business Analysis
This lesson explains the core concept model in business analysis, the different classification of requirements, various stakeholders that the business analyst work with and the comparison between requirements and designs.
Planning and Monitoring Business Analysis Activities
This lesson provides the guidelines to planning and choosing the best approach for a business analysis activity, stakeholder engagement, decision making, information management and how to measure the performance of the business analysis activity.
Eliciting Requirements & Collaborating with Stakeholders
This lesson provides insight on how to prepare and conduct for elicitation, confirm and communicate the results of your elicitation, ensure wholesome collaboration with various stakeholder groups at every point of the project.
Managing Requirements In a Project
This lesson explains the principles procedures of tracing, maintaining, prioritizing, assessing and approving requirements .
Strategy Analysis
This lesson looks at how to carry out a current state analysis to identify defects and pain points, define the future state for a solution, assess the associated risk and define the strategy that needs to be followed to implement the change.
Requirement Engineering
This lesson explains the core activities of an IT business analyst which looks at how to specify, model, verify and validate requirements, define the requirement architecture,design options and how value can be realized from the solution. .
Solution Evaluation
This lesson explains how to measure, analyze, performance of the solution, assess the limitations of the solution and that of the enterprise, and recommend actions to increase the value of a solution .
